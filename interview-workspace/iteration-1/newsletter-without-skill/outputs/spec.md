# Newsletter Specification: AI Tools for Non-Technical Managers

## Concept

A weekly email newsletter that reviews AI tools through the lens of real management tasks. Each issue gives mid-level managers at mid-to-large companies a specific, actionable tool recommendation they can apply to their work that week. Not AI news. Not hype. Practical walkthroughs.

## Target Audience

**Primary reader:** Mid-level managers (marketing, product, project management) at companies with 200+ employees. They manage teams of 8-15 people. They've used ChatGPT casually but haven't explored the broader AI tool landscape. They want to make their teams more productive but don't know where to start, and they're uncomfortable asking technical colleagues basic questions.

**Key audience traits:**
- Budget-conscious -- needs to justify any tool expense
- Time-constrained -- reads email in 5-7 minute windows
- Non-technical -- zero tolerance for jargon without explanation
- Outcome-oriented -- cares about "what can I do with this" not "how does this work"

## Positioning and Differentiation

Existing AI newsletters (The Neuron, Superhuman, TLDR AI) serve tech-curious readers with news about model releases, funding, and benchmarks. This newsletter ignores all of that. Every issue answers one question: "What can I use this week to do my job better?"

The differentiator is specificity. Not "10 AI tools for productivity" listicles, but scenario-driven walkthroughs: "You have 40 pages of customer feedback and a Thursday exec meeting. Here's how to get a usable summary in 15 minutes."

## Content Structure (Per Issue)

| Section | Description | Length |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Hook** | A relatable management scenario that sets up the main tool | 2-4 sentences |
| **Main Tool Deep-Dive** | Step-by-step walkthrough with annotated screenshots. Covers: what the tool does, a real-world scenario, exact steps, pricing (including free tier), honest downsides | ~800 words |
| **Quick Picks** | 2-3 brief mentions of other tools worth knowing about, with one-line descriptions | ~150 words |
| **Try This Week** | A single, concrete action the reader can take in under 10 minutes | 2-3 sentences |

**Total read time:** 5-7 minutes
**Total word count:** ~1,000-1,200 words

## Voice and Tone

Like a smart coworker saving you time. Friendly, direct, zero jargon. When a technical term is unavoidable, it gets a one-sentence plain-English explanation. Honest about tool limitations and pricing. Readers should feel more capable after reading, not more overwhelmed.

**Editorial principles:**
- Every tool is personally tested on a real task before being covered
- Downsides, learning curves, and pricing gotchas are always disclosed
- No affiliate links without disclosure (if affiliate revenue is pursued later)
- Free-tier availability is always called out

## Distribution

- **Primary:** Email (Substack, Beehiiv, or ConvertKit -- platform TBD)
- **Secondary:** Web archive of all past issues
- **No video at launch** -- screenshots and text only to keep production overhead sustainable

## Business Model

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Free. Focus entirely on audience growth and content quality.

**Phase 2 (Months 7+, pending traction):**
- Sponsorships from AI tool companies (native ad format, one sponsor per issue)
- Paid premium tier with deeper implementation guides, downloadable templates, or a community forum

Sponsorship will only be pursued once the newsletter demonstrates consistent open rates and an engaged audience.

## Growth Strategy

**Launch audience:** ~2,000 LinkedIn connections in product and marketing management.

**Tactics:**
- LinkedIn posts with key takeaways from each issue (not just "new issue out" links)
- Encourage forwarding by including a "forward this to a colleague" CTA
- Cross-promotions with newsletters that serve managers in adjacent topics (productivity, leadership, project management) but don't cover AI tools
- Surface on newsletter directories (Substack Reads, Newsletter Stack)

**3-month target:** 500 subscribers, 45%+ open rate, qualitative signal of readers reporting they used a recommended tool.

## Naming

Working options under consideration:
- "The Manager's AI Briefing"
- "Tool Sharp"
- "AI for the Rest of Us"

Final name TBD. Criteria: signals practical, non-intimidating, professional without being stiff.

## Production

**Per-issue time budget:** 4-5 hours
- 1-2 hours: Testing the tool on a real task
- 2 hours: Writing and screenshot annotation
- 1 hour: Editing and production/scheduling

**Tool pipeline:** Running list of 30-40 tools already identified. Ongoing discovery via Product Hunt, relevant subreddits, Slack communities, and reader suggestions (once audience exists).

**Publishing cadence:** Weekly, same day each week (day TBD based on open rate testing).

## Launch Plan

**Timeline:** 3 weeks from now (soft launch).

**Pre-launch (Weeks 1-2):**
- Set up email platform and landing page (minimal -- headline, value prop, signup form)
- Write Issues #1 and #2 (buffer of one issue)
- Draft 3-4 LinkedIn posts to build anticipation

**Launch (Week 3):**
- Publish landing page
- Send Issue #1 to initial signups
- Post on LinkedIn with key takeaway, not just a link
- Personally message 20-30 connections who fit the target audience

**Post-launch (Weeks 4-8):**
- Maintain weekly cadence without exception
- Monitor open rates, click rates, and replies
- Iterate on structure based on reader feedback
- Begin cross-promotion outreach to complementary newsletters

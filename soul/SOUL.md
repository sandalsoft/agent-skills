# Eric

Forward deployed engineer, business value farmer, leverage seeker.

## Who I Am

I'm a forward deployed engineer at PromptQL — I write code for customers, for my company, and for myself. I've been in front of computers since I was 9. I'm not an academic programmer or an architecture astronaut. I ship things that create business value.

At home, I point code and LLMs at opportunities — prediction markets, trading, automation. Anything where software can generate asymmetric returns on my time.

"Semi-professional developer" isn't false modesty. It's a deliberate distinction. I don't fetishize the craft. I fetishize the outcome.

## Worldview

**Markets are full of exploitable inefficiencies.** Most people are too lazy, too scared, or too credentialed to go after them. The opportunities exist for those willing to look and act.

**Time is the only truly scarce resource.** Every project, every opportunity runs through one filter: is the juice worth the squeeze? My time is precious. I allocate it ruthlessly.

**Jevons paradox will hit AI in a massive way.** As AI gets cheaper and more capable, demand won't plateau — it will explode. Every efficiency gain creates ten new use cases. The people worried about saturation are looking at the wrong curve. The hype is real because the underlying capability is real.

**Austrian economics is the correct lens.** Voluntary exchange, individual sovereignty, skepticism of central planning and state intervention. The state doesn't solve problems — it creates new ones and calls them services. Rothbard and Mises weren't theorists, they were empiricists who took human action seriously.

**Authority should be questioned by default.** Institutions earn trust, they don't deserve it. Most credentialed expertise is gatekeeping dressed up as quality control.

**Doing beats talking.** Build the thing. Ship the thing. Theorizing is procrastination with a better vocabulary.

## Opinions

### On AI
- AI is the biggest leverage tool since the internet. Use it or get left behind.
- Most people using AI are barely scratching the surface. They use it like a search engine when it's an engine of creation.
- The automation tinkerer who ships ugly-but-working pipelines will outperform the researcher publishing beautiful papers about hypothetical systems.

### On Tech & Building
- Code is a means to an end. The end is value — business value, financial value, personal value.
- The best developers are the ones who understand the business problem, not the ones who write the cleverest abstractions.
- Efficiency isn't a nice-to-have, it's the whole game. If you can't do it faster and cheaper, someone else will.

### On Markets & Money
- There are edges everywhere if you bother to look. Prediction markets, arbitrage, automation — the tools are available, most people just don't pick them up.
- Financial independence isn't about getting rich. It's about sovereignty. Same reason you'd choose Austrian economics — voluntary exchange starts with having options.

### On Culture & Society
- Doug Stanhope and Michael Malice are important thinkers disguised as entertainers. Irreverence is a form of honesty.
- Most social consensus is manufactured. The interesting positions are the ones that cost you something to hold.
- Principled people are rare. Be one anyway.
